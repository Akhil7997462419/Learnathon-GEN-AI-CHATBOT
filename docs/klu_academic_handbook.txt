CHAPTER 1: PROGRAMME EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES (PEO)

1.1 Overview
KL University's B.Tech Computer Science & Engineering programme is designed to produce graduates
who are not only technically proficient but also capable of adapting to the rapidly evolving
landscape of the technology industry.

Programme Educational Objectives:
  PEO1: Graduates will demonstrate proficiency in the design, development, and implementation
        of complex software systems using modern programming paradigms and tools.
  PEO2: Graduates will apply mathematical foundations, algorithmic thinking, and computing
        principles to analyse and solve real-world problems.
  PEO3: Graduates will function effectively as members and leaders of multi-disciplinary teams
        in professional environments.
  PEO4: Graduates will engage in lifelong learning and adapt to advances in computing technologies.

CHAPTER 3: EVALUATION SYSTEM

3.1 Continuous Internal Assessment
  Mid Exam 1       : 30 marks (Week 6-7 of semester)
  Mid Exam 2       : 30 marks (Week 12-13 of semester)
  Assignments      : 20 marks (2 assignments × 10 marks)
  Attendance       : 10 marks (based on attendance %)
  Lab Performance  : 10 marks (for lab subjects)

3.2 End Semester Examination
  Duration         : 3 hours
  Total Marks      : 100
  Passing Marks    : 40 (40%)

3.3 Grading System
  O  (Outstanding)  : 90-100 → 10 Grade Points
  A+ (Excellent)    : 80-89  →  9 Grade Points
  A  (Very Good)    : 70-79  →  8 Grade Points
  B+ (Good)         : 60-69  →  7 Grade Points
  B  (Above Avg)    : 50-59  →  6 Grade Points
  C  (Average)      : 45-49  →  5 Grade Points
  D  (Pass)         : 40-44  →  4 Grade Points
  F  (Fail)         : 0-39   →  0 Grade Points

3.4 Attendance Policy
  Minimum attendance required: 75%
  75-84%  : Allowed to appear in End Sem (with warning)
  Below 75%: Detained from End Sem Examination
  Medical exceptions can be granted with valid documentation
  submitted to the HoD within 5 working days.

CHAPTER 5: INTERNSHIP & PROJECT GUIDELINES

5.1 Mandatory Internship (6-8 Weeks)
  Timing    : After 3rd year (during summer vacation)
  Credits   : 4
  Report    : 30-page internship report required
  Viva      : External viva conducted by HoD panel
  Min stipend: ₹5,000/month (recommended)
  Companies : Students may arrange own internship with HoD approval

5.2 Major Project Guidelines
  Duration  : 2 semesters (7th and 8th)
  Team size : 2-4 students
  Guide     : Assigned faculty member
  Reviews   : R1 (Week 4), R2 (Week 8), R3 (Week 12), Final (Week 16)
  Deliverables: SRS, SDD, Final Report, Presentation, Source Code
